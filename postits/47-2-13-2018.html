The ways in which a user interacts with design can be studied and recorded through cognitive science. Rather than seeing the brain as a conglomeration of neurons and synapses, cognitive science analyses conceptual processes that receive, digest and more rarely reiterate stimuli. The Model Human Processes neatly describes this phenomenal series through a set of processors. This model is particularly useful because it judges human interactions and cognitive speeds to accurately predict human behaviour. This predictability can further be scrutinised for optimisation and efficiency adjustments.
