Today I took a break from my typical coding routine of going through more JavaScript and Java materials, and I decided to try something new. I did this in part because it's always great to expand horizons, but I'll be honest in saying I wanted something more tangible. I didn't just want to see static pages or console outputs. I wanted to animation and whole new level of interactivity. Today, I tackled Unity, and I almost loved it.

Unity s a heavyweight game engine for 2D and 3D games. Since Unity was announced just over twelve years ago at Apple's Worldwide Developer Conference, it has grown from being supported just on iOS systems to twenty-seven different platforms. Some examples include Mac, Windows, Linux, WebGL, Playstation systems, Xbox, some Nintendo systems, just to name a few. Some of the popular games that have been created on it include Temple Run, Plauge Inc., Tiger Woods, Family Guy, Slender Man, Cypher, Sonic, Angry Birds, the list goes on. The software is full of built-in features, a market of plugins, an increasingly recognisable layout and a bunch of other features I have yet to learn. It allows for scripting to be written in C#--another caveat of today.

I started looking into Unity because I think there's a lot of potential with mapping and Unity. I've seen tweets of people using real map data to create games, but I'm interested in what Unity can do to with real world map data. I certainty don't have an answer yet as to what it could do.
