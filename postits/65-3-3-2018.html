Earlier today, a recruiter said they ask all their applicants, "What's something you've learned recently?" They emphasized learning that was beyond technical skills--something more empathetic. I've been trying to think of what I'd talk about if ever asked. Sustainable food systems? That relates to people worldwide! or what about Playing the cello? Music is a great way to self-express! But those answers didn't feel right. They feel impersonal, like they weren't really answering the question--a superficial answer to a question that could be leagues deep. I'm not sure if this is appropriate to tell a recruiter, but the most important lesson I've learned recently is multi-faceted. I learned how to be a good caretaker. I learned how to take care of a dying person and those around them. I learned how to be strong even when I'm not. I learned about sacrifice. I learned how to be an advocate against doctors. I learned about life. I learned about death. I learned about cancer. I learned how the body works and what happens when it doesn't. I learned about time. I learned about limited time. I learned about value. I learned how to value. I learned how to mediate family. I learned; I learned; I learned. I learned so much beyond these simple sentences. These lessons can't be taught, only learned. While this topic might not be the most appropriate for a recruiter, I think they are among the most important lessons I will ever learn.
