Stone Game

In this game, there are two players that alternate turns with Player 1 always starting. During each turn, a player collects either 2, 3 or 5 stones. For simplicity, the game is played optimally, not strategically. If there are 2 stones, then Player 1 will collect the two and there will be no stones left for Player 2, so Player 2 loses. If there are 9 stones, then Player 1 will collect 5 stones, Player 2 collects 2 stones, then Player 1 will collect the last 2 stones. Player 2 then loses the game.

The part of the pattern to focus on is the minimum collection of 2 and maximum collection of 5. Imagine there are n stones. If a player is to collect 5 and have a remainder of 2, then x is 7. Because the turn alternation is stationary, this algorithm can be scripted around the number 7. If n % 7 == 0 || n % 7 == 1, then Player 2 will win. If the remainder is any other number, then Player 1 will win.
